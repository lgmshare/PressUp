Low blood pressure, also known as hypotension, is a condition where the blood pressure is lower than normal. While it is not always a concern, it can cause dizziness, fainting, and even be life-threatening in some cases. In this article, we will discuss the causes, symptoms, and management of hypotension.
Causes of Hypotension
The causes of hypotension range from dehydration to serious medical conditions. Some of the common causes of hypotension include:
  • Dehydration
  • Heart problems and heart valve conditions
  • Hormonal problems such as underactive thyroid (hypothyroidism), diabetes, or low blood sugar (hypoglycemia)
  • Certain medications such as antihypertensive drugs
Symptoms of Hypotension
Low blood pressure might cause no noticeable symptoms, or it might cause dizziness, fainting, confusion, cold and clammy skin, decrease in skin coloration (pallor), rapid and shallow breathing, weak and rapid pulse, and blurred vision.
Management of Hypotension
If hypotension is causing symptoms, the treatment depends on the cause. For instance, if medication causes hypotension, your healthcare provider may recommend changing or stopping the medication or lowering the dose. If it's not clear what's causing hypotension or no treatment exists, the goal is to raise blood pressure and reduce symptoms. Depending on age, health, and the type of hypotension, there are several ways to do this:
  • Use more salt: Experts usually recommend limiting salt (sodium) because it can raise blood pressure, sometimes dramatically. For people with hypotension, however, that can be a good thing. But too much sodium can lead to heart failure, especially in older adults. So it's important to check with a healthcare provider before increasing salt.
  • Drink more water: Fluids increase blood volume and help prevent dehydration, both of which are important in treating hypotension.
  • Wear compression stockings: These help reduce the pooling of blood in the legs and improve circulation, which can help raise blood pressure.
  • Medications: Your doctor may prescribe medications to help treat hypotension. These drugs help to treat orthostatic hypotension, such as fludrocortisone, which helps to raise blood volume, and midodrine, which helps to narrow blood vessels to raise blood pressure.
Conclusion
Understanding the causes, symptoms, and management of hypotension is important for taking control of your health. By making lifestyle changes and working with a healthcare provider, it is possible to prevent or manage hypotension and reduce the risk of serious health complications.
